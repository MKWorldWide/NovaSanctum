# NovaSanctum Source Review Checklist

Effective date: 2026-02-07

Use this checklist before promoting a resource into a pathway.

## 1. Resource Identity

1. Title is captured accurately.
2. Source/publisher is identified.
3. Canonical URL is valid and reachable.

## 2. Access and Licensing

1. Resource is freely accessible (no required paywall for pathway completion).
2. License or usage terms are documented.
3. Rights status is clear enough for educational curation use.

## 3. Credibility and Relevance

1. Source credibility is acceptable for the pathway level.
2. Resource aligns to at least one explicit pathway outcome.
3. Resource prerequisites match target learner level.

## 4. Currency and Stability

1. Publication or update date is recorded when available.
2. Content is not obviously outdated for the subject area.
3. URL stability is acceptable (not a temporary or volatile source link).

## 5. Accessibility and Inclusion

1. Resource is readable/usable on common devices.
2. Alternative formats are noted when available (text/video/transcript).
3. No unnecessary barriers to access (account lock-in, region restrictions where avoidable).

## 6. Safety and Policy Compliance

1. Content does not include harmful or unverifiable claims presented as fact.
2. AI-related content aligns with `/Users/sovereign/Projects/NovaSanctum/docs/AI_ASSISTANCE_POLICY.md`.
3. Resource and framing align with NovaSanctum mission and nonprofit positioning.

## 7. Metadata Completeness

Each accepted resource includes:

1. `title`
2. `source`
3. `url`
4. `license` (or explicit note when unknown and pending verification)
5. `domain`
6. `level`
7. `estimatedHours` (if applicable)
8. `prerequisites` (if applicable)
9. `curationStatus`

## 8. Decision

1. `APPROVE` (publishable)
2. `APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS` (requires listed remediation)
3. `REJECT` (off-mission, noncompliant, or low-quality)
