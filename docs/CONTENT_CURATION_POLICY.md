# NovaSanctum Content Curation Policy

Effective date: 2026-02-07

## 1. Objective

Ensure NovaSanctum curates reliable, accessible, and legally usable educational resources aligned to public-interest learning goals.

## 2. Eligible Source Types

1. Open educational resources (OER)
2. Public documentation and open course materials
3. Publicly accessible textbooks and lectures
4. Government, university, and nonprofit educational publications

## 3. Source Selection Criteria

Resources should be evaluated for:

1. Accessibility (free access, minimal barriers)
2. Credibility (recognizable source, clear authorship)
3. Relevance (aligned to roadmap outcomes)
4. Currency (reasonably up to date for the domain)
5. Licensing clarity (usage rights are clear)

## 4. Attribution Standard

Each curated item must include:

1. Title
2. Source or publisher
3. URL
4. License or usage terms when available
5. Last reviewed date

## 5. Exclusions

NovaSanctum does not include:

1. Paywalled materials as required pathway dependencies
2. Unverifiable or speculative claims presented as educational fact
3. Content that conflicts with safety, legal, or ethical standards
4. Materials with unclear provenance or rights status

## 6. Review and Maintenance

1. New resources undergo curation review before publication.
2. Existing resources are rechecked on a recurring cycle.
3. Broken links, outdated materials, and licensing conflicts are corrected promptly.

## 7. Quality Control

1. Use structured templates for pathway entries.
2. Require outcome alignment for each resource block.
3. Record curation decisions to support auditability and grant review.
