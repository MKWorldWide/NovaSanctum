# NovaSanctum Website Structure Recommendation

Effective date: 2026-02-07

## 1. Purpose

Provide a clear, grant-legible public information architecture for NovaSanctum that is educational, institutional, and non-speculative.

## 2. Recommended Information Architecture

1. Home
2. Mission and Governance
3. Learning Domains
4. Pathways
5. Resource Navigator
6. AI Assistance Policy
7. Impact and Reporting
8. Partners and Related Initiatives
9. Compliance and Contact

## 3. Page-Level Content Intent

1. Home
   - One-paragraph mission statement, institutional guardrails, pathway entry links.
2. Mission and Governance
   - Charter, curation policy, governance workflow summary, review cadence.
3. Learning Domains
   - Plain-language domain descriptions and intended learner outcomes.
4. Pathways
   - Structured entry-to-outcome pathway cards with prerequisites and estimated effort.
5. Resource Navigator
   - Search and filtering for open resources with curation status indicators.
6. AI Assistance Policy
   - Allowed/disallowed AI functions and human oversight commitments.
7. Impact and Reporting
   - Practical metrics: pathway starts, progression checkpoints, completion patterns, source health.
8. Partners and Related Initiatives
   - Explicit boundary text for GameDin as outreach/community, not institutional authority.
9. Compliance and Contact
   - Security policy, code of conduct, responsible disclosure, and program contact.

## 4. Tone and Framing Standards

1. Use institutional and plain language.
2. Avoid speculative claims, founder-centric messaging, and antagonistic framing.
3. State non-replacement position clearly: NovaSanctum complements formal education.

## 5. Publication Controls

1. New public pages require governance workflow approval.
2. Policy and pathway pages should include last-updated dates.
3. Any AI-generated or AI-assisted copy is human-reviewed before publication.
